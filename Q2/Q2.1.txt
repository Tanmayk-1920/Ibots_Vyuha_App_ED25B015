A way to explain stack and heap is to take the example of a toy lego tower and a toy box.

Toy Lego Tower (Stack):
The Stack is like a tall , lego tower. You can only add another lego at the top and can only remove from the top one at a time.
Because everything in stack is a neat , straight line, the computer knows where the next toy lego is, at the very top making it very fast.
When you are done with the toy , you just remove the top legos making it very easy to clean.(Happens automatically by computer)
But your tower can only be so tall , if it is too high , it hits the ceiling of the room and crashes , this is called stack overflow.


Toy Box (Heap):
The Heap is like a giant, messy toy box. If you want to play with a giant toy racetrack, it won't fit on your Lego tower. So, you throw it into the big toy box. You can put things anywhere you want of any size.
But because you put things anywhere , the computer does not exactly know where the toy is , so it is slower.
The box can only fit so many things , if it is too full, any new tous you put just overflow (Memory Leak) ,so remember to remove unnecesssary toys from the box (Does not happen automatically , manually frreing up memory is important).
